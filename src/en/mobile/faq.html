FAQ

What is PlatON?

PlatON is a next-generation global computing network and a public infrastructure in the new digital age. It is therefore not only a blockchain, but a service-oriented operator for the coming future. PlatON proposes a brand new computation model for providing the full range of computing, storage and communications capabilities based on advances in distributed computing, cryptography and blockchain technology as well as social progress over the last decade. All basic resources such as computing power, algorithms and data are provided as services. PlatON will provide an open source infrastructure and services to all distributed application developers, resource providers as well as communities, organizations and individuals with computation requirements.

Will PlatON go open source?

Yes. When the PlatON network goes online, all of the code will be made open source.

What are PlatON’s features and strengths?

PlatON proposes a completely new computing architecture that decouples computation from consensus. It implements an innovative off-chain approach to improving linear computing scalability, uses Verifiable Computation (VC) to prevent fraudulent computation; and adopts parallel computing to enhance computing performance.
PlatON is a decentralized and distributed RPC framework. On the RELOAD overlay network PlatON implements a P2P service discovery mechanism which supports dynamic service discovery and registry, service transparent routing and access, service containers with multi-lingual support, as well service orchestration and service governance capabilities.
PlatON is the first operational service network in the world to provide full privacy protection. Extensive use of cryptographic algorithms is made to protect the sovereignty and privacy of data. As data protection for individual users continues to increase in importance, PlatON provides data subjects and data controllers with a new way of data sharing based on collaborative computing, which avoids the risks of data leaks and abuse.

Will there be a PlatON client?

Edge is the common cross-platform (including Web, Android and iOS platforms) client offered by PlatON for all users to access the PlatON network. The design of Edge will follow the style of social networks to provide users with an easy-to-use interface. All functions, such as IM, wallets, Dapp Portal and front-end application run-time environment, will be supported.

How can developers get involved?

PlatON fully supports the launching of new projects based on the platform by the global developer communities. We are sincerely inviting developers from every field to develop different plug-ins, algorithms, services and applications on PlatON. They can also develop application chains targeted at certain technical directions or vertical industries. We will provide developers with an easy-to-use development environment, a service market and operation support. We will also incubate and invest in all kinds of applications, services, and other business activities based on PlatON through our ecology fund. Interactions and collaborations between different applications and application chains will help build a unified global complex network and ecosystem.

What can PlatON do?

The PlatON platform provides a public infrastructure for collaborative computing of global data. Secure application chains and applications for data exchange can be established on PlatON to provide institutions and individuals with safe and reliable data exchange and collaborative computing services. These help to protect data privacy during data sharing and gain economic benefits without losing ownership. All types of applications centered around data exchange and computation will be able to enjoy the convenience of global data collaboration and computing power sharing through PlatON. These include global scientific computing, data transactions, medical data clusters, and joint credit investigation.
Based on PlatON P2P networks, all types of decentralized applications can be established. Decentralized identity verification, social networking, exchanges, payment clearing and settlement can operate independently of centralized institutions. Advantages include decentralization, privacy protection, security, and incentive compatibility.
PlatON can also effectively solve problems brought by IoT development, such as big data management, trust, security and privacy. PlatON provides large-scale IoT networks with infrastructure that offers data security and privacy protection, and edge computing capability based on the computing power sharing platform, which will accelerate the development of IoT applications in fields such as smart manufacturing, Internet-of-Vehicles, agriculture, smart cities, and distributed energy management. Also innovations in business models will be promoted.
